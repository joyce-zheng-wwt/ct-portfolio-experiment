// The Process

How the
strand
thinks.

I
Ingest your repository
HELIX parses your AST, indexes semantics, maps dependencies, and builds a living model of your codebase. Not chunked text — structured understanding.
II
Reason about intent
Every request is contextualized against your architecture before a single token is generated. HELIX asks: what is this system trying to do? What would fit?
III
Generate with precision
Code output matches your conventions, naming patterns, error-handling style, and test coverage requirements — because it learned them from you.
IV
Validate and iterate
HELIX runs your tests, interprets failures, and self-corrects — completing the loop without switching context or writing a single prompt.

// Why We Built This

Intelligence woven

Every great codebase has a double helix at its core: the logic of what it does, and the intent of why it exists. Most AI tools only read one strand. HELIX was built to hold both — and to write from that deeper understanding.
